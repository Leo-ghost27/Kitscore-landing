FAQ

Questions sponsors ask
before they buy.

What actually counts as "verified" evidence?

A campaign is marked Verified only after both sides confirm it — the creator submits proof (screenshots, deliverables, platform data) and the sponsor who ran the campaign confirms it happened as described. Self-reported claims are shown as self-reported, never blended into a verified score.

What stops a creator from gaming their self-reported data?

Self-reported data is labelled as such everywhere it appears and carries no weight in the Verified Reputation score. It only becomes evidence once a real sponsor confirms it happened, which requires that sponsor's own account and campaign history — not something a creator can fabricate alone.

What do I get for the $29 evaluation?

A full score breakdown across five components, a risk and brand-safety assessment, audience fit analysis against your campaign brief, an AI-generated decision brief, and a downloadable sponsor decision memo PDF.

Is the Trust Score preview really free?

Yes. The Trust Score and Confidence Rating are visible on every creator profile with no payment or account required to view them. You only pay when you want the full evaluation and decision memo.

How is this different from tools that score creators from scraped social data?

Kitscore's core score is built on mutually confirmed campaign history and sponsor endorsements, not inferred solely from public posts. The AI brief summarizes that verified evidence — it doesn't replace it.
