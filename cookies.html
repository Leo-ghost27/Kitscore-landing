Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

This is a working draft matching Kitscore's current cookie usage. It is not legal advice — have a lawyer review it if you later add analytics or advertising cookies, since that would require a consent banner under GDPR/UK PECR rules.

1. What this covers

This policy explains how Kitscore (operated by GHG Consulting LLC) uses cookies and similar technologies (such as browser local storage) on kitscore.co and our app.

2. The cookies we use

TypePurposeCan you opt out?
Essential / authenticationKeeps you signed in to your Kitscore account and keeps your session secure (set by Supabase Auth)No — required for the Service to function. You can avoid them by not signing in.
Essential / securityProtects against fraud and abuse of forms and checkoutNo — required for the Service to function.

We do not currently use analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking cookies. We don't use cookies to build advertising profiles, and we don't sell or share cookie data with advertisers.

3. Local storage

In addition to cookies, your browser may store small amounts of data locally (for example, session tokens used by Supabase Auth) to keep the app working smoothly between page loads. This data stays on your device and is used the same way as the essential cookies above.

4. Third-party cookies

Payments are processed by Stripe, which may set its own cookies during checkout for fraud prevention and to process your payment securely. These are governed by Stripe's own cookie policy, not ours.

5. Managing cookies

Because Kitscore currently only uses essential cookies, there's no separate cookie consent banner — blocking these cookies in your browser settings will likely prevent you from signing in or completing checkout. If we add analytics or advertising cookies in the future, we'll update this policy and add a consent banner before doing so.

6. Changes to this policy

If our cookie usage changes — for example, if we add analytics — we'll update the date at the top of this page and add a consent notice where required.
