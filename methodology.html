The trust score

A number between 0 and 100.
Backed by verified data.

The Kitscore Trust Score is a weighted composite of five components, each scored 0–100. The final score reflects not just performance, but the quality of evidence behind each component. A live-verified data point carries more weight than a self-reported one.

Trust Scores are recalculated automatically whenever a creator updates their profile, uploads new evidence, answers the brand safety questionnaire, or has a campaign confirmed by a sponsor.

Why not just use follower counts? Follower counts measure size, not quality. A creator with 10K highly-engaged, brand-safe, UK-based followers is often more valuable to a sponsor than one with 200K followers of uncertain authenticity. Kitscore measures what actually matters to sponsorship outcomes.

Confidence rating

The score tells you what.
Confidence tells you how sure.

Every Trust Score ships with a Confidence Rating — a percentage that reflects how much of the score is backed by verified data versus self-reported information. A score of 80 with 90% confidence is meaningfully different from a score of 80 with 40% confidence.

Confidence increases as creators upload evidence, get verified by Kitscore, and have campaigns mutually confirmed by sponsors. It decreases if evidence is missing, outdated, or self-reported only.

Low confidence
0 – 49%
Mostly self-reported data. Profile is incomplete or evidence is missing. Score should be treated as indicative only.
Medium confidence
50 – 79%
Mix of verified and self-reported data. Some evidence submitted. Score is reasonably reliable but room for verification remains.
High confidence
80 – 100%
Majority of components live-verified or evidence-backed. At least one confirmed campaign. Score can be relied upon for sponsorship decisions.
Verification sources

Three tiers of data.
Not all evidence is equal.

Kitscore weights data differently depending on how it was verified. Live-verified data carries the most weight. Self-reported data carries the least — but still contributes, because any information is better than none.

Brand safety

Eight checks.
Every creator, every time.

Brand safety is scored via a structured 8-question assessment. Each question is answered by the creator and can be supplemented with content review evidence. Unfavourable answers apply calibrated score penalties — the severity reflects real sponsor concern levels.

01
Adult content
Presence of explicit or sexually suggestive content in the creator's primary content channels.
02
Gambling promotion
History of promoting gambling, betting, or gaming products without appropriate disclosure.
03
Political content
Frequency of political commentary or advocacy that could polarise a brand's customer base.
04
Profanity
Regular use of strong language that may conflict with brand tone or family-safe requirements.
05
Family safety
Whether the creator's content is appropriate for a broad, family-inclusive audience.
06
Paid disclosure (FTC)
Consistency of disclosing paid partnerships. Failure to disclose is a legal and reputational risk for sponsors. Highest penalty weighting.
07
Misinformation history
History of sharing content later identified as false or misleading by authoritative fact-checkers.
08
Controversy history
Past viral controversy events — major or minor — that generated significant negative public attention.

Reliability Score

Not just who they are.
How they deliver.

The Reliability Score is Kitscore's primary differentiator. While the Trust Score measures a creator's audience and content quality, the Reliability Score measures their track record as a sponsorship partner — based entirely on verified, mutually-confirmed campaign data.

No other platform has this. It cannot be gamed, purchased, or self-reported. It is computed exclusively from campaigns that both the creator and sponsor have independently confirmed.

Formula: Campaign completion rate (30%) + Would Hire Again % (30%) + Average sponsor ratings across communication, professionalism, and deliverable quality (25%) + Repeat sponsor rate (15%). Capped at 100. Recomputed automatically on every new confirmed campaign or endorsement.
